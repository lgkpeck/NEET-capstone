### Conflicting Variables and Resolution <strong>Engineering Variables:</strong> <br>Time to heat molten salt brick <br>Insulation material <br>Insulation thickness <br>Removability of insulation <br>Surface area of brick <br>Volume of brick <br>Type of salt <br>Initial temperature of molten salt brick <br>Duration of molten salt brick as heating source <br>Cost of insulation <br>Cost of brick <br>Surface temperature of brick Currently, the most difficult conflict of variables relates to the material placed around the molten salt brick. Insulation around the brick will enable easier transport of the brick, and it will also prevent heat loss from the brick during transportation. However, this insulation may also result in a low enough surface temperature such that the brick surrounded by insulation provides insufficient heating for the home. Furthermore, it will take longer to heat a brick surrounded by insulation. One resolution to this conflict may be to develop removable insulation for the brick.
